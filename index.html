Data Visualization

The Five Pillars of Data Visualization

Alberto Cairo has graciously transmitted the 5 integral laws that dictate what a good data visualization must do. He is not the originator of these necessary qualities and attributes them to the enlightened Enrico Bertini. For a data visualization to be orthodox, it must adhere to the following:

  1. It is truthful, as it's based on thorough and honest research.
  2. It is functional, as it constitutes an accurate depiction of the data, and it's built in a way that lets people do meaningful operations based on it.
  3. It is beautiful, in the sense of being attractive, intriguing, and even aesthetically pleasing for its intended audience.
  4. It is insightful, as it reveals evidence that we would have a hard time seeing otherwise.
  5. It is enlightening because if we grasp and accept the evidence it depicts, it will change our minds for the better.

The Enlightening

The above infographic shows the percentage of U.S. population by age group. It gracefully displays three axes with a series of two dimentional images. Instead of haveing the z-axis depicted in one image, the artist made the sensible decesion of using time passing to display, you guessed it, time passing. With the clean presentation, one's mind is open to inquire about the implications and causes of the data. Why did the baby boom happen? Why do females an average outlive males? Why didn't the baby boom lead to a subsequent baby boom from the babies boomed?It thoroughly expands the island of knowledge and doesn't create a confusing fog that obfuscates the known and how to reach the unknown.
